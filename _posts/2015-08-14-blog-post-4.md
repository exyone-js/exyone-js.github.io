_最近在寻找合适的云服务器时，我发现了一个不错的平台——酷鸭数据(Kooya)。如果你也在寻找性价比高且连接稳定的海外服务器，不妨了解一下这个选择。_

## 平台简介

酷鸭数据的官网是 https://www.kooya.hk，平台提供中文界面，对国内用户相当友好。这家服务器的老板是印尼人，来中国留学过，在印尼本土也提供云服务，所以还算是比较稳的吧。

他们家在印尼总部的官网:[_node.id_](http://node.id)  
注册可以使用我的推介链接:[_https://www.kooya.hk/recommend/2f0BBo4mIqSr_](https://www.kooya.hk/recommend/2f0BBo4mIqSr)

## 服务器配置介绍

### 节点分布：

-   中国香港节点
    
-   新加坡节点
    

选择节点一定要选延迟低的，可以联系客服测一下延迟。节点在亚太地区的优势在于无论是国内还是国外网络环境，都能稳定连接到服务器，这对于需要跨境访问的项目来说非常省心。

### 可选套餐：

目前平台主要提供三种套餐配置：

-   **Gold6133**处理器配置
    
-   **E5 2673V4**处理器配置
    
-   E5建站促销套餐
    

### 重要提醒：

虽然Gold处理器版本性价比相对较低，但它提供原生IP，这对于需要搭建邮局或跨境电商等依赖原生IP的服务至关重要。如果你有此类需求，务必选择Gold版本，并记得提交工单联系商家或直接找客服开放25端口。

## 操作系统选择指南

平台提供多种操作系统选择：

-   **Windows Server**：只提供了旧版本系统
    
-   **Debian**：最轻量节省资源，适合低配置服务器（首选）
    
-   **Ubuntu Server**：功能最全面强大，软件生态丰富，推荐新手使用（次选）
    
-   **CentOS**：以稳定性和安全性著称，适合企业级应用，但需要注意的是CentOS传统版本已停止更新，建议选择替代分支
    
-   **Fedora**：提供的是旧版本，无法安装**最新软件**（千万别选，除非是把云服务器当作远程Linux使用）
    

## 使用体验与注意事项

### 访问与登录：

连接稳定：国内外网络环境均可稳定连接，体验流畅。

极致便捷：支持直接用邮箱注册和登录，省去记忆复杂用户名或绑定手机的麻烦，非常方便。

客服与价格：

客服响应：有时比较慢或者会不回复，记得在不同时间段多叫一下客服。

服务优势：服务器价格相对便宜，适合预算有限的个人用户或小项目，并且因为这家之前就有在亚太地区的服务，尽管在中国的服务刚起步但还算放心。

### 平台功能限制：

目前不支持在线更改套餐配置，购买时需谨慎选择。（客服回应正在计划开发）

管理界面中规中矩，称不上美观但实用性足够。（希望能改改前端界面）

网页SSH功能尚不完善（界面相当简陋粗糙，不能自定义，有时会卡住），建议使用本地SSH工具（如Xshell、Finalshell）进行连接，体验更佳。

## 总结

酷鸭数据作为一个海外云服务平台，在价格、易用性和网络稳定性上都表现不错。其邮箱直接登录的便利性和国内外稳定连接的特性，使其特别适合刚入门的个人开发者、需要运行小型项目或寻求稳定跨境连接的用户。虽然在某些功能上有所限制，但其原生IP优势对于有特定需求（如开放25端口）的用户来说是一个很好的选择。

_So, I was recently on the hunt for a decent cloud server and stumbled upon this platform called Kooya Data. If you're also scouting for an overseas server that's easy on the wallet and offers stable connections, you might want to give this one a look._

## What's Kooya All About?

Their website is https://www.kooya.hk, and it's fully in Chinese, which is pretty handy if that's your preferred language. Rumor has it the owner is from Indonesia, studied in China, and runs a cloud business back home too—so the operation seems somewhat reassuring.

Their company’s official website at the Indonesia headquarters: [node.id](node.id)  
You can register using my referral link: [https://www.kooya.hk/recommend/2f0BBo4mIqSr](https://www.kooya.hk/recommend/2f0BBo4mIqSr)

## What's Under the Hood?

### Server Locations:

-   Hong Kong **PRC**
    
-   Singapore
    

Picking the right node is key—go for the one with lower latency. You can always ask their support for a quick ping test. Having nodes in Asia is a plus; they tend to deliver stable connections whether you're accessing from inside or outside China, which is a major headache-saver for any cross-border projects.

### Plans You Can Pick From:

Right now, they've got three main options:

-   The Gold 6133 Processor plan
    
-   The E5 2673V4 Processor plan
    
-   An E5 "Website Special" promo plan
    

### Heads Up:

The Gold plan might not be the best bang for your buck, but it comes with a native IP—a must-have if you're setting up things like a mail server or running cross-border e-commerce shops. If that's your jam, definitely go for Gold, and don't forget to file a ticket (or ping support directly) to get port 25 unblocked.

## Picking an OS? Here's the Lowdown

They've got a few choices:

-   **Windows Server**: Only older versions available.
    
-   **Debian**: Super lightweight and easy on resources. My top pick for lower-spec servers.
    
-   **Ubuntu Server**: Packed with features and has great software support. Solid choice for beginners.
    
-   **CentOS**: Rock-solid and secure, great for serious projects. Just note that the classic version isn't updated anymore—better go with one of the alternatives.
    
-   **Fedora**: They only offer older releases, so you can't get the latest software. I'd skip this one unless you're just using the server as a remote Linux box.
    

## My Experience & a Few Caveats

### Getting Started:

Connection: Stable from both within and outside China. No major hiccups here.

Sign-up: Dead simple—just use your email. No need to remember a username or link a phone number. A nice touch.

### Support & Pricing:

Customer Service: Can be hit or miss—sometimes they reply fast, sometimes... not so much. Try messaging at different times if you don't get an answer.

The Good Stuff: Prices are pretty competitive, great for individuals or small projects on a budget. Since they've been around in Asia for a while, I felt okay trying them out even though their China-facing service is newer.

Where They Could Do Better:

No option to upgrade/downgrade your plan from the dashboard yet. Choose wisely from the start. (Support says it's in the works.)

The admin panel gets the job done but isn't winning any beauty contests. (Hey Kooya, maybe a UI refresh?)

The web-based SSH terminal is… pretty basic (and occasionally glitchy). You're better off using a local tool like Xshell or Finalshell.

## Wrapping It Up

All in all, Kooya Data is a promising player—affordable, user-friendly, and reliably connected. The email login and solid performance from anywhere make it a great fit for solo devs, small projects, or anyone who needs steady cross-border access. It's got a few rough edges, but if you need a native IP (with port 25), it's definitely worth considering.
